Try These Prompts

Each step has a prompt designed to keep the thinking yours. Copy, adapt, paste into your Claude Project.

Step 1 — From Interest to a Question

Try This Prompt · Essayez cette consigne
"Don't propose a topic. Ask me one question at a time to sharpen my interest into a specific, answerable research question."
« Ne propose pas de sujet. Pose-moi une question à la fois pour affiner mon intérêt en une question de recherche précise et répondable. »

Step 2 — Set Up the Project

Project Instructions · Consignes du Projet
"This project supports my thesis: [question]. Be my critical reading partner. Use only sources I upload — never invent citations. Flag what you're unsure of."
« Ce projet porte sur ma thèse : [question]. Sois mon partenaire de lecture critique. Utilise seulement les sources que je téléverse — n'invente jamais de références. Signale ce dont tu n'es pas sûr. »

Step 2 — Map Search Terms

Try This Prompt · Essayez cette consigne
"Break my question into core concepts. Give me synonyms and 5 query strings for Google Scholar and arXiv, plus the subfields and venues where this work appears."
« Décompose ma question en concepts clés. Donne-moi des synonymes et 5 requêtes pour Google Scholar et arXiv, et les revues où paraît ce travail. »

Step 2 — Interrogate the Corpus

Try This Prompt · Essayez cette consigne
"Build a table comparing these papers by method, workload type, key metric, and limitation. Where do they disagree? What hasn't been tested yet?"
« Construis un tableau comparant ces articles par méthode, type de charge de travail, métrique clé et limite. Où sont les désaccords ? Qu'est-ce qui n'a pas encore été testé ? »

Step 3 — Identifying the Gap

Try This Prompt · Essayez cette consigne
"Map the contending approaches and where they disagree. Do not tell me my gap — just lay out the landscape."
« Cartographie les approches concurrentes et leurs points de désaccord. Ne me dis pas quelle est ma lacune — décris simplement le paysage. »

Step 4 — Scoping & Feasibility

Try This Prompt · Essayez cette consigne
"Ask me about my data, tools and time before suggesting anything, then help me judge what is realistic."
« Interroge-moi sur mes données, outils et contraintes de temps avant de suggérer quoi que ce soit, puis aide-moi à juger ce qui est réaliste. »
These prompts keep AI in the asking role.
The answers — and the gap claim — are yours.
Step 2 · S'orienter dans le domaine

Orienting to the Field — 5 Moves

All five moves happen inside a single Claude Project. The project becomes your persistent research workspace — not a chat session, a configured environment.

01
Set up the Project Claude Project
  • Create one Project for the whole thesis — not per-chat, per-thesis
  • Put your research question and short context in the Project instructions
  • Tell Claude how to behave: critical reading partner, no invented citations
Project Instructions
"This project supports my thesis: [question]. Be my critical reading partner. Use only sources I upload — never invent citations. Flag what you're unsure of."
02
Map search terms Claude
  • Ask Claude to break the question into core concepts and synonyms
  • Get ready-to-paste query strings for Google Scholar, arXiv, IEEE Xplore
  • Name the subfields and venues where this work typically appears
03
Gather sources You do this
  • Run queries in Google Scholar, arXiv, Semantic Scholar, your library
  • Use "cited by" and "related articles" to snowball outward
  • Keep the most relevant; judge which matter — that judgment is yours
Do This Yourself · À faire vous-même

Claude doesn't fetch papers — and shouldn't pretend to. Finding sources, and judging which are relevant, stays with you.

04
Build the set Upload to Project
  • Upload what you gathered into the Project as knowledge
  • Now Claude is grounded in your real sources — not its parametric memory
  • Ask: for each paper — problem, method, key result, limitation in 3 lines; then name the 5 most central to your question
05
Interrogate & keep a living log Claude
  • Have Claude compare sources and surface where they disagree
  • Keep a running log in the Project — update it as new papers arrive
  • Ask: what hasn't been tested for your specific question?
Keep the Thinking Yours · Gardez la réflexion

Claude can invent references — trust only sources you uploaded or verified. You decide what is relevant and where the gap is. Check every claim against the actual paper. Can you explain and defend this source map as your own understanding?

Core Principles · Principes directeurs

Why HOW We Use AI Matters

Research has two purposes. The first is to create new knowledge — the thesis, the paper, the result. The second is to learn to think as a researcher — the skill the degree certifies. AI can accelerate the first while quietly eroding the second, unless you use it deliberately.

The Guiding Principle

Let AI absorb

Friction that doesn't teach

Finding papers · Formatting & translating · Summarizing for orientation · Organizing references

Preserve for yourself

Friction that does teach

Forming your own question · Judgment & critical evaluation · Deciding where the gap sits · Defending your argument

The Litmus Test

Can you explain and defend this output as your own thinking?
Pouvez-vous l'expliquer et le défendre comme votre propre réflexion ?

If you can answer yes, you've used AI well. If you can't, you've let it do the thinking. That is not a moral failure — it's a workflow miscalibration. The five steps are designed to keep you on the right side of that line.

What You Leave With

  • 01 A sharpened research question — Une question de recherche affinée
  • 02 An organized literature base — Une base documentaire organisée
  • 03 A configured AI workspace — Un espace de travail IA configuré
