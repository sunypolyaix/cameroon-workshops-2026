# Chrome Extensions for a Graduate Student AI Research Platform

*A browser-based guide for researchers using Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini.*

---

## The Core Idea

Don't lose your thinking. AI conversations are where ideas happen — where you work through a problem, stumble onto a framing, or get a question back that reorients your whole approach. This stack treats those conversations as primary research material, worth saving, organizing, and returning to.

This is a browser-based platform designed for Chrome on a laptop or desktop. If your primary device is a phone, the workflow looks different and would require a separate guide.

---

## The Extensions

### Conversation Exporters
**Claude Conversation Exporter · ChatGPT Exporter · Gemini Chat Exporter**

These three are the foundation. Every substantive conversation you have with an AI model should be exported and saved — to your local drive first, with Google Drive as a backup. Don't curate aggressively at first; save broadly. You are building an archive of your own intellectual process. A conversation from months ago may contain an idea you abandoned prematurely, or a framing that suddenly fits a new problem. You can't go back to what you didn't save.

Export in Markdown format where possible. Markdown files can be read by humans and fed back into any model without friction — unlike PDF, which is harder to reuse.

A simple folder structure is all you need:

```
/research-archive/
  /claude/
  /chatgpt/
  /gemini/
```

Export, give the file a recognizable name, drop it in the right folder. When you've accumulated a semester's worth, you can upload an entire folder to Gemini and ask it to synthesize across sessions — surfacing themes, contradictions, or ideas you may have lost track of. You can also expose your old conversations to new literature you've gathered, asking the model to find connections you didn't see at the time.

**Export as soon as possible while you have a connection.** Sessions can time out, accounts can have issues, browsers can crash. Don't assume the conversation will be there later.

---

### Usage Trackers
**Claude Usage Tracker · ChatGPT Usage Limit Tracker · Gemini Usage Extension**

Each model has usage limits. Knowing where you are in a cycle lets you pace your work — save intensive synthesis sessions for when you have headroom, and use lighter tasks when running low. For a researcher juggling coursework, projects, and writing, this is practical resource management.

---

### Claude QoL — Export, Fork, Search
**Claude QoL 2.7.3**

This extension deepens your Claude practice specifically. Forking a conversation lets you explore a tangent without abandoning your main thread. Search makes a long working session navigable after the fact. Bookmarks turn key moments in a conversation into reference points. Think of it as giving Claude a better interface for sustained intellectual work — less chat window, more research environment.

---

### Zotero Connector

This is your literature intake point — the entry to a structured reference library that complements your unstructured conversation archive. One holds the canonical literature, the other holds your evolving thinking about it. Capture sources when you encounter them, not retroactively. A single click saves a citation with full metadata intact.

---

### Markdown Reader

Small but useful. As your workflow matures, you will produce and share more `.md` files — notes, summaries, exported conversations. Being able to preview Markdown natively in the browser keeps the workflow tight.

---

### Google Docs Offline

For researchers in contexts with unreliable electricity and intermittent internet, this extension is not a convenience — it is infrastructure. When power or connectivity drops, your work should not stop. Google Docs Offline means anything you were writing continues to be editable and syncs automatically when the connection returns.

Enable this proactively, before you need it.

---

### Authenticator

Not research workflow — basic security. If you are using institutional accounts (Google, Zotero, GitHub), two-factor authentication protects everything else in this stack. Keep it enabled.

---

## The Platform Logic

Over time, the archive becomes an asset. The goal is not to build a perfect system — it is to not lose the thinking you have already done. A semester of exported conversations, saved to Google Drive, can be uploaded to a model with a large context window and asked to surface what you've been circling without realizing it. Pair that with literature captured in Zotero and you have something genuinely useful: a model reading your intellectual history alongside your current sources.

This is not about AI doing your research. It is about having access to your own thinking when you need it.
