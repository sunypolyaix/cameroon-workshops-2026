# Core Principles

## Why Research Has Two Purposes

Research is not only about producing a result. It has two distinct purposes, and both matter:

**1. Create new knowledge** — the visible product: the thesis, the paper, the dataset, the result. AI can help you get here faster and more thoroughly.

**2. Learn to think as a researcher** — the skill the degree certifies. The ability to identify a problem, situate it in a field, locate a gap, and construct an argument. This is what AI can quietly erode if you let it do the thinking.

*C'est pourquoi la manière d'utiliser l'IA est essentielle.* This second purpose is why HOW you use AI matters as much as whether you use it.

---

## The Guiding Principle

> Let AI absorb friction that doesn't teach. Preserve friction that does.

### Let AI absorb — friction that doesn't teach

- Finding papers
- Formatting and translating
- Summarizing for orientation
- Organizing references and notes

### Preserve for yourself — friction that does teach

- Forming your own question
- Judgment and critical evaluation of sources
- Deciding where the gap sits
- Constructing and defending your argument

The distinction is not about effort. Some tasks are effortful without being instructive — those belong to the machine. Some tasks are difficult precisely because they require your judgment — those stay with you.

---

## The Litmus Test

> Can you explain and defend this output as your own thinking?

*Pouvez-vous l'expliquer et le défendre comme votre propre réflexion ?*

Apply this test to every AI-assisted output before you use it. If you can answer yes — if you can walk someone through why this source matters, why this gap is real, why this question is worth answering — you have used AI well. If you cannot, you have let it do the thinking. That is not a moral failure; it is a workflow miscalibration. The five steps are designed to keep you on the right side of that line.

---

## What You Leave With

At the end of a properly scaffolded first-steps session, a researcher should have:

1. **A sharpened research question** — specific, answerable, defensible as their own
   *Une question de recherche affinée*

2. **An organized literature base** — sources gathered and verified by the researcher, uploaded and queryable
   *Une base documentaire organisée*

3. **A configured AI workspace** — an environment set up to support the thesis over time, not just a conversation
   *Un espace de travail IA configuré*

These are not outputs the AI produces. They are outputs the researcher produces, with AI as an instrument.
