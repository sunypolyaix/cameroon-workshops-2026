# 0-claude-framing.md — From Interest to a Question

## Tool
Claude (claude.ai) · Socratic question-sharpening method

## Prompt used to configure Claude

> "Don't propose a topic. Ask me one question at a time to sharpen my interest into a specific, answerable research question."

## Session — Gisèle · University of Buea, Electrical Engineering

| Speaker | Message |
|---------|---------|
| Gisèle | I want to work on the interconnection of the Northern Interconnected Grid in Cameroon with the Chad National Power Grid. |
| Claude | Interesting area. What pulls you most — the physical engineering of the connection itself, or the planning and policy side of making it happen? |
| Gisèle | The engineering — specifically the technical challenges of making the grids work together. |
| Claude | When you think about what could go wrong technically, what concerns you most — power losses along the line, voltage instability, or frequency control? |
| Gisèle | All three, but I think the starting point is choosing where to connect — the busbar selection — because that determines everything else. |
| Claude | And what would make one busbar a better choice than another — is it minimizing losses, keeping voltage in range, or something else? |
| Gisèle | Minimizing losses is the primary goal, but voltage levels and frequency have to stay within acceptable limits too. |

## The question Gisèle arrived at

> Interconnection of the Northern Interconnected Grid (NIG) [Cameroon] with the Chad National Power Grid — determining the busbars that will yield minimal power losses, adequate voltage levels, and frequency within acceptable limits. This project will focus on analyzing the technical, economic, and strategic challenges of this interconnection with the Chadian grid, leveraging artificial intelligence tools to propose innovative and sustainable solutions to enhance service quality, stability, and reliability of the power system.

## Why this is a research question

It is specific (busbar selection), multi-criteria (losses, voltage, frequency), bounded (NIG–Chad corridor), and names the method (AI-assisted optimization). Every element came from Gisèle's answers, not from Claude.
