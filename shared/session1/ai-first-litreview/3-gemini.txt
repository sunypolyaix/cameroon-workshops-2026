Wu et al. demonstrate that African grid interconnections are essential for building a low-carbon future by unlocking wind and solar potential. Imdadullah et al. highlight that interconnecting grids provides massive economic benefits by stimulating sustainable growth and balancing supply/demand mismatches efficiently.

The Need for Strategic Siting and Infrastructure: None of the papers suggest building power systems arbitrarily. Wu et al. use a "multicriteria assessment" to balance transmission distance, cost, and environmental footprint when siting power infrastructure. Imdadullah et al. emphasize that choosing the right transmission technology (e.g., HVAC vs. HVDC) is vital for ensuring grid stability and managing short-circuit faults. Kelly et al. optimize specific generating configurations based on the localized daily load profiles of different Chadian regions.

While the sources overlap in their goals, they approach the problem from completely different scales, technological focuses, and methodologies. You can use these distinct lenses to structure the "Technical," "Economic," and "Strategic" chapters of your review.
