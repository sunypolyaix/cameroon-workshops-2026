5 Interrogate the sources NotebookLM
What NotebookLM does

Document-grounded only

You upload your PDFs. The model is constrained to those sources — it cannot draw from its training data. Every answer is cited to a specific passage. It cannot confabulate.

Why it matters here

Gemini read everything together and mapped the landscape. NotebookLM lets you verify specific claims against specific text. Ask it something Gemini said and confirm whether the papers actually support it.

Audio overview

NotebookLM can generate a podcast-style conversation summarizing your sources — useful for orientation and for students who absorb material by listening.

The key feature

Most AI tools expand outward — connecting your question to everything they know. NotebookLM contracts inward — staying inside the literature you gave it. That constraint is the point.

Your reflection — after NotebookLM

What question emerged that you did not have before this session? What did the sources not answer?

Take notes in Claude alongside NotebookLM. Export the conversation.

7 Synthesize your conversations Gemini · Long context

After 25–35 sources: collect every exported conversation from steps 1–6. Upload them all to Gemini. Ask for synthesis at three scales.

The synthesis ladder

25 words

The single most important thing that emerged across all your conversations.

100 words

The argument you have been building, in your own terms, with context.

400 words

The full shape of your thinking — themes, tensions, where you landed and where you are still uncertain.

Then iterate: ask Gemini what changed between your first conversation and your last. Ask it what you kept returning to. Ask it what you never resolved.

The question this step answers

After all of this — what do you think?

This is the synthesis that belongs in your thesis introduction, not the model's summary of the papers.
