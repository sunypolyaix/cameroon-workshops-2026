# What You Cannot Hand Off

The tools are fabulous. Use them for everything that can be specified — retrieval, organization, extraction, cataloging.

> **LLMs give you organized material to think with. The thinking is yours.**

That is not a limitation to work around. It is the condition of doing research. You will get there faster, and on a firmer foundation, if the organizational work is done right — which is exactly what this workflow is for.

---

## What LLMs Cannot Independently Warrant

### 1. Gap claims

Gaps are defined by *absence*. The model lives inside the corpus. You stand outside it.

An LLM that has been trained on a field will reproduce that field's blind spots. It cannot identify what is absent because its training signal comes entirely from what is present. Absence produces no training signal. The gap that would make your thesis contribution is precisely the thing the model cannot see.

### 2. Novel synthesis

LLMs interpolate — they produce outputs consistent with the existing distribution of their training data. Novel synthesis means moving to a position the existing literature has not occupied. That movement requires a researcher who can hold two frameworks in tension and see what emerges from their combination. A model will average them.

### 3. Productive contradictions

LLMs smooth contradiction. When two sources disagree, a model tends to produce a synthesis that makes both partially right. The intellectual work of a literature review often lives in the incommensurability — in the place where two approaches cannot both be correct and the gap between them is the opening for new work.

### 4. The time it takes

Phase 4 of serious research — the gap identification and analytical synthesis — takes 60–100 hours on a significant research question. There is no shortcut. The workflow in this session saves time on the 60–80 hours of volume work that precedes analysis. It does not compress the analysis itself.

## The Warrant Statement

Use this framing when you write:

> *The AI-assisted workflow produced an organized set of candidate references and grounded per-paper notes. Gap identification, source selection, and the synthetic argument in Section [X] are my own.*

This is accurate. It gives credit to the tools without overclaiming, and it clearly locates the intellectual contribution.

## A Final Note on AI Tools in Your Institution

The tools demonstrated in this workshop — Gemini, NotebookLM, Zotero — are free at the tier we used. You do not need institutional access, a paid subscription, or special permissions to begin.

What you need is a research question (Step 1 from the first session gives you that), a Google account (for Gemini and NotebookLM), and a few hours to work through the verification step carefully.

The workflow scales. Start with the 3–5 papers you verified in this session. Run the Paper Extractor Gem on each one. You will have a structured knowledge base for your thesis topic by the end of the week.
