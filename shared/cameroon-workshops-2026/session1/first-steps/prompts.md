# Prompts to Try

Copy, adapt, paste. Each prompt is designed to keep AI in the asking or scaffolding role — not the answering role. Adapt the bracketed sections to your own topic.

---

## Step 1 — From Interest to a Question

**What it does:** Configures your AI workspace as a Socratic sparring partner. It asks, you sharpen. It never proposes a topic.

**Try This Prompt · Essayez cette consigne**

> "Don't propose a topic. Ask me one question at a time to sharpen my interest into a specific, answerable research question."

> « Ne propose pas de sujet. Pose-moi une question à la fois pour affiner mon intérêt en une question de recherche précise et répondable. »

**How it works:** The AI asks a single question. You answer. It asks another based on your answer. After 3–5 rounds, you will have a question that is genuinely yours — because you answered every step.

---

## Step 2a — Set Up the Project

**What it does:** Establishes the rules of engagement for your entire research workspace. Paste this into your Project instructions (Claude) or at the start of each session (Gemini).

**Project Instructions · Consignes du Projet**

> "This project supports my thesis: [your question here]. Be my critical reading partner. Use only sources I upload — never invent citations. Flag anything you're unsure of."

> « Ce projet porte sur ma thèse : [votre question]. Sois mon partenaire de lecture critique. Utilise seulement les sources que je téléverse — n'invente jamais de références. Signale ce dont tu n'es pas sûr. »

---

## Step 2b — Map Search Terms

**What it does:** Breaks your question into searchable components. Gives you ready-to-paste query strings for the databases you have access to.

**Try This Prompt · Essayez cette consigne**

> "Break my question into core concepts. Give me synonyms and 5 query strings ready to paste into Google Scholar and arXiv. Name the subfields and key venues where this work typically appears."

> « Décompose ma question en concepts clés. Donne-moi des synonymes et 5 requêtes prêtes à coller dans Google Scholar et IEEE Xplore. Indique les sous-domaines et les revues où ce travail paraît. »

---

## Step 2c — Interrogate the Corpus

**What it does:** After you have uploaded your gathered papers, asks the AI to compare them structurally and surface where they disagree or leave questions open.

**Try This Prompt · Essayez cette consigne**

> "Build a table comparing these papers by method, workload type, key metric, and limitation. Where do they disagree? What hasn't been tested yet for [your specific context]?"

> « Construis un tableau comparant ces articles par méthode, type de problème, métrique clé et limite. Où sont les désaccords ? Qu'est-ce qui n'a pas encore été testé pour [votre contexte spécifique] ? »

**Important:** Only works reliably if you have uploaded the actual papers. If you ask this without your papers uploaded, the AI draws from its training data — and may confabulate.

---

## Step 3 — Identifying the Gap

**What it does:** Asks the AI to map the landscape of approaches — without telling you what your gap is. That judgment stays with you.

**Try This Prompt · Essayez cette consigne**

> "Map the contending approaches and where they disagree. Do not tell me my gap — just lay out the landscape."

> « Cartographie les approches concurrentes et leurs points de désaccord. Ne me dis pas quelle est ma lacune — décris simplement le paysage. »

**Why the constraint matters:** An AI that names your gap is doing the work that your thesis committee will evaluate you on. It can map presence. You identify absence.

---

## Step 4 — Scoping and Feasibility

**What it does:** Forces the AI to understand your actual constraints before it suggests anything. Prevents recommendations that are theoretically correct but practically impossible.

**Try This Prompt · Essayez cette consigne**

> "Ask me about my data availability, tools, and time before suggesting anything. Then help me judge what is realistic for a [thesis / master's project / 6-month study]."

> « Interroge-moi sur la disponibilité des données, les outils et le temps dont je dispose avant de suggérer quoi que ce soit. Puis aide-moi à juger ce qui est réaliste pour une [thèse / mémoire / étude de 6 mois]. »
