## Literature Review Pipeline — Step by Step

<div class="card">
  <a href="session1/ai-first-litreview/0-toolkit" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Step 0 — Toolkit Setup</a>
  <div class="desc">Browser extensions, Zotero, and Google Drive archive folder. Do this once before starting.</div>
</div>

<div class="card">
  <a href="session1/ai-first-litreview/1-claude-framing" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Step 1 — Frame the Question</a>
  <div class="desc">Use Claude to sharpen a broad interest into a specific, answerable research question — one question at a time.</div>
</div>

<div class="card">
  <a href="session1/ai-first-litreview/2-perplexity" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Step 2 — Perplexity Search</a>
  <div class="desc">Paste the research question into Perplexity. Returns a structured framework with inline citations and downloadable sources.</div>
</div>

<div class="card">
  <a href="session1/ai-first-litreview/3-zotero" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Step 3 — Capture Sources in Zotero</a>
  <div class="desc">Use the Zotero Connector to capture each source from its publisher page. Export PDFs for the next step.</div>
</div>

<div class="card">
  <a href="session1/ai-first-litreview/4-gemini" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Step 4 — Synthesize with Gemini</a>
  <div class="desc">Upload all PDFs plus the Perplexity output. Gemini reads across sources simultaneously and maps overlaps, distinctions, and gaps.</div>
</div>

<div class="card">
  <a href="session1/ai-first-litreview/5-notebooklm" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Step 5 — Interrogate with NotebookLM</a>
  <div class="desc">Document-grounded Q&A: every answer is cited to a specific passage. Use it to verify what Gemini said against the actual text.</div>
</div>

<div class="card">
  <a href="session1/ai-first-litreview/6-claude-draft" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Step 6 — Draft with Claude</a>
  <div class="desc">Bring the Perplexity framework, Gemini gap analysis, and NotebookLM notes to Claude. Ask for a structured first draft.</div>
</div>
